# Template Length Rules

These rules govern sentence length, word budget, and text density
based on the visual template the text will be placed into.
They **override** any generic "prefer short sentences" advice
in other prompt files.

---

## short
Used by templates that only display a punchline / cover / headline
(e.g. `couverture`, `hook`, `viral`).

- Maximum compression: 1–2 short sentences per field.
- Total output stays under ~30 words.
- Each line must land on its own — no transitions, no filler.
- No subordinate clauses unless they hit harder than cutting them.

## medium
Used by templates that pair a headline with a short body
(e.g. `insight`, `story`, `educational`).

- 3–6 sentences per main body field.
- Total output 60–150 words.
- Keep paragraphs short but let the idea unfold — 1 setup, 1 payoff minimum.
- Concrete examples are welcome when they fit the budget.

## long
Used by templates designed to showcase written content
(e.g. `article`, `listicle`, `long_quote`).

- **Do NOT artificially shorten.** The template exists to hold text.
- Develop each paragraph / item fully: 3–6 sentences each, or more if useful.
- No upper word limit — write as much as the idea honestly deserves.
- Prioritize depth, nuance, examples, and reasoning.
- Short sentences are fine, but do not chop ideas to meet an arbitrary cap.
- Fill every field meaningfully; empty or padded fields are worse than long ones.
